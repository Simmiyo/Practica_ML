Properness of associated minimal surfaces
We prove that for any open Riemann surface $N$ and finite subset $Z\subset
\mathbb{S}^1=\{z\in\mathbb{C}\,|\;|z|=1\},$ there exist an infinite closed set
$Z_N \subset \mathbb{S}^1$ containing $Z$ and a null holomorphic curve
$F=(F_j)_{j=1,2,3}:N\to\mathbb{C}^3$ such that the map $Y:Z_N\times N\to
\mathbb{R}^2,$ $Y(v,P)=Re(v(F_1,F_2)(P)),$ is proper.
  In particular, $Re(vF):N \to\mathbb{R}^3$ is a proper conformal minimal
immersion properly projecting into
$\mathbb{R}^2=\mathbb{R}^2\times\{0\}\subset\mathbb{R}^3,$ for all $v \in Z_N.$
